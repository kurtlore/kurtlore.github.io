TAYLOR SWIFT ANNOUNCES RERECORDING OF 4TH ALBUM

Taylor Swift, otherwise known as the music industry, announces the rerecording of her fourth studio album, 'Red,' releasing on November 12, 2021. This marks her second rerecorded album in her journey to own the master recordings of her songs.

With a tracklist of thirty songs, Red (Taylor's Version) introduces ten 'from the vault' songs from Swift, along with the original tracklist of 'Red' rerecorded nine years later.

She collaborated with Phoebe Bridgers, Ed Sheeran, and Chris Stapleton for three of the vault songs.

Taylor welcomed Carole King to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame ceremony with her rendition of 'Will You Still Love Me Tomorrow,' on October 31, 2021. Donning a stunning lace bodysuit, Swift performs the song in a mesmerizing synth-pop sound with her iconic dreamy voice as an homage to the singer-songwriter pioneer.

Seven years ago, hearts were collectively broken when Taylor Swift performed her critically-acclaimed song, 'All Too Well,' at the 56th Grammy Awards. Swift made everyone feel her pain as she belts the song from the top of her heart. Her emotion-fuelled performance continues to be one of her best acts.

With a discography of over 200 songs spanning numerous genres, it is impossible not to find a song that you can't love.
