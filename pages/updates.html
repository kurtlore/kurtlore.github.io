Taylor Pays Tribute to Carole King at Rock Hall of Fame

Taylor welcomed Carole King to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame ceremony with her rendition of 'Will You Love Me Tomorrow' on October 31, 2021.

Donning a stunning lace bodysuit, Swift performs her rendition in a mesmerizing synth-pop sound with her iconic dreamy voice as an homage to the singer-songwriter pioneer.

Taylor had Carole in tears with her performance that could easily slip on 1989 with the dreamy synth sounds of 'Wildest Dreams' and 'You Are in Love' and lush backing vocals.

She continues on the program with her speech about her idol and inspiration, King. “Her persona on 'Tapestry' feels like listening to a close friend intimately sharing the truths of her life so that you can discover the truths of your own," Swift praises.

Swift went on to present King a spot at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Saturday as part of the Class of 2021, with the likes of Jay-Z and the Foo Fighters.

King and Swift have previously met at the 2019 American Music Awards when King awarded Swift the Artist of the Decade award.
